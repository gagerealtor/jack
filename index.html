Our family heritage

The Jack story begins in Broussard, Louisiana — and stretches across generations to Houston, Texas.

  • 1911

    Willie Jack is born

    Born February 28, 1911 in Broussard, LA. Known everywhere as "Mr. Willie" — a knitter of caps and scarves, always there to listen and advise.

  • 1914

    Mary Landry is born

    Born July 4, 1914 in Broussard, LA. Grew up among 12 siblings. Famous for her pecan candies — mailed every Christmas from St. Martinsville to Houston.

  • 1931

    Mary & Willie marry

    United in Holy Matrimony on April 29, 1931. They settled in St. Martinsville, LA and raised their family working sugarcane and cotton fields together.

  • 1931–49

    Seven children

    Vivian, Willis, Mayola, Anita, Sidney, Stella, and Susie — each carrying Mary and Willie's love forward.

  • Today

    The family reunites

    Grandchildren and great-grandchildren keeping the legacy alive — celebrating in Houston, TX where so many of the family settled.

Memories & traditions

The stories every Jack family member should know by heart.

MARY'S TRADITION

Every Christmas, Mary mailed boxes of her famous pecan candies to her children in Houston. A small box, a very big love.

WILLIE'S GIFT

Willie hand-knitted winter caps and scarves for every child and grandchild — a man who showed love through what he made with his own hands.

HOW THEY MET

Both born in Broussard, LA — Mary and Willie grew up in the same small town before marrying on April 29, 1931 in Holy Matrimony.

THE NICKNAME GAME

Nanoon · Tee-Neg · Da' · Black Gal · Fee · Aunt Vee — every sibling had a nickname. Do you know who's who?

THE MOVE TO HOUSTON

Many Jack children settled in Houston, TX — so Mary's Christmas candy boxes had to travel by mail all the way from St. Martinsville.

MARY'S SPIRIT

"Everyone she came in contact with throughout her life loved Mary." — from the family heritage document written for the 2005 reunion.

Pre-reunion learning

Four weeks of family discovery before everyone arrives at the reunion.

  • Wk 1

    Read the family heritage story

    Share "Our Family Heritage" — the full story of Mary and Willie, their lives in Broussard and St. Martinsville, Louisiana.

  • Wk 2

    Complete the nickname quiz

    Can you match all 6 siblings to their nicknames? Share by group chat — make it a family competition with a small prize.

  • Wk 3

    Submit a memory or photo

    Each household sends one memory of Mary, Willie, or a past reunion. These get printed as cards for the big day.

  • Wk 4

    Study the family tree

    Practice filling in Mary & Willie's children and grandchildren. The day-of contest will test everyone!
